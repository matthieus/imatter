---
title: "Did Excel threaten the software industry?"
date: 2026-05-16 10:00:00 +0100
---
"Did Excel threaten the software industry?" is a retorical question with an obvious 'no' answer. The software industry has eaten the world at the same time Excel was everywhere. Excel made possible for most people to create custom software solutions where many off-the-shelf solutions would be too expensive, or just not good enough. AI-built software is making a similar promise. And I think AI isn't killing software for the same reason Excel didn't.

Excel showed to a lot of people what software could do for them. You can do anything in Excel, from trivial to very complex requirements. But implementing even the simplest requirement without shooting yourself in the foot in the process isn't trivial itself. And then maintaining that spreadsheet without shooting yourself in the head is even harder. Many billions were spent on projects started from a scaled up version of that observation.

Now don't get me wrong, AI can create the equivalent of what a spreadsheet can do in seconds. But you won't stop there. First, it won't do exactly what you asked for. And then it's very hard to not use your powers to add more, especially if it takes a few more seconds. And using that software will create more software requirements, infinitely. And quickly you end up with a similar situation as with Excel spreadsheet, you become the maintainer of a complex piece of software hiding bugs you can't or don't want to fix anymore. In effect, you've become a software maintainer, a job you never signed up for in the first place. AI is going to show, even more than Excel, what software can do for us. And humans who are ready to spend their working life taming the result will be more needed than ever.

Thinking that everyone is going to ask AI to create all the software they need is nonsense. Since I've started using AI to write code, of course I haven't asked AI to create all the software I need. Even if creating software is my job. But because I can do a lot more with AI, I also need a lot more software to help do these things. And something else, AI needs software even more than us.
