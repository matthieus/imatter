This blog is a journal of my journey building software: my reasoning, what I try, and what I ultimately learn.

The title "I Matter" must sound familiar to someone who's done some therapy, or coaching. This blog is a little bit of that, a form of therapy for myself, which I hope could also help others.

But there’s another meaning. In a world where AI can be framed as a replacement for human work, I matter is also a statement. It argues that we should flip the narratives. Instead of focusing on AI replacing a person, we focus on the person getting superpowers. People can now achieve goals previously out of reach. You don't want to replace someone who's achieving more.

This narrative flip is at the core of what I’m building. The software I’m working on is designed to help us focus on what matters and make progress toward our goals, whatever they are. This blog explores the process of building that software as well as my own experience using it.
