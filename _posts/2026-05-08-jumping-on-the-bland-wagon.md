---
title: "Jumping on the band (bland?) wagon"
date: 2026-05-08 10:00:00 +0100
---
Software is going through an AI revolution. Being a software engineer today is like being a farmer in the 18th century and yourself and your farm are transported to the 21st century overnight. You had a small field and an ox, and now you have to deal with GMOs and tractors. You're now producing huge quantities, and what you're producing can resist pests and looks objectively better. Though subjectively, it tastes bland and normalised. The AI revolution is both revolutionising how we make software, and the software itself. A double revolution.

Beside the software double revolution, I am mid-life and mid-career, and I cannot ignore my internal revolution anymore. Voices telling me to start trusting in me have accumulated half my life, and mid-life does imply another half-life to go. I've got to do something. This triple revolution gets me in a perfect storm for my middle-aged self to start the startup adventure. My situation isn't unique. I am aware many are making that exact same jump, at a similar time in their life. And the problem I want to solve is being solved by many. But let's look at ecosystems in nature, there is an infinite way to solve the same problems, and thrive. And we have a whole new ecosystem to fill. And hopefully my attempts won't be too normalised, or bland.
